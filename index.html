Lettering Guide
Typographic font roles for the Coloring Book Series
Purpose: This guide defines how words in Bible verse coloring book posters should be treated typographically. It ensures consistency across the series — the same word hierarchy, design logic, and spiritual intent — regardless of illustration style. For use in this coloring book series only. Do not share or repurpose outside this project.
Keep in mind
  • All words are rendered at the same size — hierarchy comes from typeface style, weight, and posture only.
  • Script words read together in sequence must form a positive, uplifting thought.
  • Bold words read together in sequence must deliver the correct core message of the verse.
  • All words are in outline form for coloring — avoid ultra-thin strokes, especially in script treatments.
  • This guide is for this coloring book series only — do not share or repurpose outside this project.

thin/light weight, noticeably thinner strokes — one family per verse, always different from Sans-serif
Within a single verse, all Sans-serif words share one font and all Understated words share one font — they don't change word to word. Both roles rotate independently through their five options verse to verse, and Understated is always a distinctly lighter weight than whichever Sans-serif font is active — so the two roles stay visually distinguishable even in outline. The table below tells you exactly which font applies to each verse.
Font role reference
Script01
thanksgiving
The felt word. Handwriting. Warm, personal, devotional. Script words read together must form a positive thought.
e.g. prayer · thanksgiving · God
Bold02
PRESENT
The declared word. All caps. Commands and divine names. Bold words read together must deliver the right message.
e.g. PRESENT · REQUESTS
Serif03
situation
The grounded word. Mixed case, bold serif, upright. Important nouns and theological terms.
e.g. situation · petition · brothers
Sans-serif04
anything
The supporting word. Montserrat, Raleway, Nunito Sans, Poppins, or DM Sans — medium weight, one family per verse. Context words that step back visually.
e.g. anything · every · requests
Italic05
anxious
The gentle emphasis. Playfair Display, EB Garamond, Cormorant Garamond, Lora, or DM Serif Display — italic, one family per book. A whisper where bold is a declaration.
e.g. anxious · petition · always
Understated06
but · in · by
The connective word. Light/Thin weight from Montserrat, Raleway, Nunito, Poppins, or Quicksand — always lighter than Sans-serif. Present to be read, not to be noticed.
e.g. do · not · be · but · in · by
The two rules — check every verse before finalising
  • Script words read in sequence must form a positive, uplifting thought on their own.
  • Bold words read in sequence must deliver the core instruction of the verse clearly and correctly.
